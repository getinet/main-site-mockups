FileMaker Development

A recognized leader in FileMaker development, Beezwax has built over one thousand FileMaker solutions for clients that include Fortune 500 business units, non-profit organizations, academic institutions, and government agencies.

An industry leader

Beezwax is a Platinum member of the FileMaker Business Alliance. Beezwax staff regularly speak at industry events, make widely-used development tools and offer ongoing workshops that teach other FileMaker developers the tricks of trade.

FileMaker and JavaScript

Beezwax is blazing new trails with integrating JavaScript with FileMaker. We were one of the first to start to integrate JavaScript components into FileMaker layouts, and share our work with the developer community via blog posts starting in 2012. As the FileMaker Platform has evolved, weâ€™ve continued using JavaScript to enhance our FileMaker solutions in meaningful ways. JavaScript running in Web Viewers can be used to replace native UI elements such as sliders or lists, add new ones such as d3 data visualizations, or even replace the UI completely.

Our FMAjax module facilitates easy two-way communication between web viewers and FileMaker without the use of plugins. JavaScript can also be used more directly using our bBox plugin.

  • FileMaker System Redesign

    If your FileMaker system is a few years old and/or has been worked on by less-than-top-notch developers, it is worth considering re-design, rather than extending. While this may seem scary and is to some extent tossing away work that has been done -- not really. Part of the value of FileMaker is that it is readily portable, especially to a new FileMaker context. Of course, the data comes along for the ride. As well, much of the structure can be ported into the new system fairly easily. So, we leverage what is there, while building a new foundation, more ready for modern technique and possibilities, and more able to capture the business needs as they are today... and tomorrow.

    interested

  • Migrating To Latest FileMaker Version

    Moving existing FileMaker Pro databases from legacy versions to the current version. This is a great way to make use of native functionality of an ever-improving framework.

    Migration is not time-intensive, as the bulk is handled automatically. However, depending on techniques currently used, there may be some gotchas which we know how to look for. Meanwhile, many of the new features are only a potential until you implement them. And, of course, we can help with that.

    interested

  • Data Visualization

    A picture is worth a thousand words. We can translate your data into dynamic visual reports to help users more quickly understand the information.

  • Going Beyond Excel

    You could say that FileMaker is to a spreedsheet what a spreedsheet is to a text document... It is a significant additional layer of abstraction and cross-referencing. So, it is common for users and teams to come to us when they have found they are relying heavily on ever-expanding, somewhat chaotic spreadsheets. We can import those sheets, and arrange into a relational structure, with a much clearer and more-directed way of accessing and reviewing the information. Without losing the established computational logic. And, of course, now all this can be easily shared with colleagues -- each leveraging the parts of the information that apply to their job function.

    interested

  • FileMaker and Web Integration

    For a decade and a half, we've been on the leading edge of integrating FileMaker and the web. In 2002, we won an award for FileMaker Inc, for developing a new tool for tying FileMaker to the web. Ever since, we've made the most of the tools that FileMaker Inc, our peers in the community, and we ourselves have invented to achieve a performant experience having portions of a FileMaker system accessed through a browser.

    We call this a FileMaker-web hybrid system, and brings the best of FileMaker's rapid development efficiencies and the web's vast reach and ease of deployment.

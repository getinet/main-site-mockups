Functional Areas

There are many kinds of narratives and organizing principles. Science is driven by evidence gathered in experiments, and by the falsification of extant theories and their replacement with newer, asymptotically truer, ones.

  • Account Management

    v1 Managing sales and relationships with clients and customers.

    v2 Managing information, transactions, communications and relationships with clients and customers. Effective account management leads to increased sales and loyal customers.

  • Accounting

    Involves the recording, processing, analysis, and reporting of the financials of a business or other organization.

  • Administration

    Managing operations and logistics, including people and other key resources.

  • Advertising

    Working the media and the messages, to support marketing initiatives.

  • Asset Management

    Includes the management of tangible and digital resources, generally in a database system.

  • Big Data

    Larger data sets require new methods to help with discovery, analysis, storage/search, querying and visualization of that data.

  • Billing/Invoicing

    Making sure payments are solicited and processed efificently.

  • Business Continuity

    Organizations analyze risk, aim to reduce vulnerabilities, and look to improve their ability to continue operations following disaster or disruption.

  • Calendaring

    Organizing time.

  • Communications

    Systems supporting the work of people who manage communications, both internal and external.

  • Compliance

    Organizations seek to achieve regulatory compliance by gathering information and understanding of the legal landscape governing their industry. Systems and procedures can be developed to manage the information and projects revolving around Compliance.

  • Contact Management

    The process and system of managing data and transactions of all contacts for an organization to facilitate things like sales and marketing.

  • Content Management

    Encompasses a broad array of tasks and tools for creating, storing, retrieving and editing content. Much of the web is driven by content management systems.

  • Contract Management

    Business organizations make agreements with other businesses and individuals. The resulting contracts need to be created, negotiated, documented, tracked, enforced and revised.

  • Credit Card Processing

    Processing payments is a fundamental part of E-commerce. Credit card and other payment processing involves interaction with payment gateways and authorization services and requires high levels of security for the safety of customer information.

  • Curriculum

    Curriculum includes the content, resources, and processes that support learning in an education or business training environment. Custom software can help develop, manage and track such curriculum.

  • Customer Relationship Management

    Manage the details of customers and track sales and service interactions. The goal is often to improve the relationship and, as a result, increase sales and customer retention.

  • Document Generation

    Generating documents, for distribution or storage, as well as managing related data, assets and workflow.

  • Document Management

    Storing and tracking documents, along with managing related metadata, assets and workflow.

  • Email Marketing

    We design and develop visual and process solutions for Email marketing for all type of clients and campaigns.

  • Event Management

    Includes end-to-end development and tracking of large events, including concept, branding, promotion, scheduling, budgeting, and site and resource coordination.

  • Facilities Management

    Involves the management of the spaces and infrastructure of an organization or business and the people and activities that take place in them.

  • Finance

    Supporting the organization's financial structure by managing things like funding, capital, analysis and forecasting.

  • Forecasting

    Forecasting uses data to predict potential outcomes as part of the basis for assessing risk and uncertainty. Forecasting is a vital component of organizational decision making.

  • Fundraising

    The acquisition of material and financial resources from donors to fund initiatives of the organization. A.K.A. SHOW ME THE MONEY.

  • Globalization

    Creation of software that helps manage localization and internationalization of content.

  • Hardware Engineering

    Is the iterative process involved in the design and testing of technologica products. By necessity, hardware engineering must work closely with software and electrical engineering processes.

  • Human Resources

    Involves the management of people, generally employees, and their relationship to the business organization. HR departments are tasked with hiring decisions, scheduling, internal policy development and enforcement, external compliance and benefit development and administration.

  • IT Services

    All the wonderful, magical things you can do with technology rely upon the underlying hardware, software and networking, and a plan to manage it all.

  • Incident Tracking

    Is the record of the details, involved parties and communication trail with respect to events that occur and potentially need to be reported.

  • Information Security

    Is the broad discipline tasked with ensuring the physical and digital security of stored information, with the aim of preventing access, theft, manipulation, duplication and extortion. Information security practices can be applied to systems used by teams in the enterprise, and also to hardware and software product development.

  • Intellectual Property

    Oversees the handling of copyrights, trademarks, patents, and other legal rights of value to an organization.

  • Inventory Management

    Manage tangible items -- track quantity and details such as description, cost, exact location. Get insight into the flow and availability of inventory and interface with sales systems.

  • Issue Tracking

    Software systems can manage tasks and communication, ensuring that the people involved with a project have good grasp of who is doing what and when.

  • Laboratory Management

    Beakers and potions and things that see really, really, really small things. It's a lot to keep track of. Oh, and those white coats.

    But seriously, labs are complex entities requirement management of supplies, equipment, personnel, projects, workflow, research, intellectual property and compliance.

  • Legal

    Advisors and advocates on all legal subjects, from contracts and IP, through compliance and litigation.

  • Legal Notices

    Building, documenting and tracking official work output and communications by Legal department experts.

  • Manufacturing

    The process of taking raw materials and products and transforming them into something new to supply to the market. Manufacturing can include a mix of human and technological solutions and ranges from simple to complex. Manufacturing process is subject to a constant drive toward improvement of efficiency.

  • Market Research

    Research to collect data, both quantitative and qualitative, to aid in decisions about marketing initiatives.

  • Marketing

    The activities associated with promoting an organization or event. Marketing encompasses branding, advertising, print media, digital media and advertising in all of its forms. Effective marketing leads to sales and customer retention.

  • Office Management

    Handling a diverse set of tasks which, depending on the office size and organization, can be vary as to administrative and operational scope.

  • Operations

    Operations, broadly defined, typically executes the functional strategy for an organization. This includes everything from development and design through production, manufacturing and logistics.

  • Ordering Systems

    Systems supporting purchasing, procurement and global supply management.

  • POS

    Point of sale (POS) solutions help a business process customer purchases and payments, both standalone (e.g. cash register) or in conjunction with larger E-commerce systems. POS also typically integrates with other systems, including inventory, invoicing, fulfillment/shipping, CRM.

  • Partnership Management

    Managing sales partnerships, co-marketing and related B2B contracts.

  • Print Production

    Print production takes creative designs, especially digital, and streamlines the process for the physical production of things like cards, brochures, books, and even 3-D materials. Custom software can help manage the projects, resources and logistics for this type of production.

  • Process Design

    Defining the planning, measurement and refinement of business processes with a goal to improve strategy, resources and operations across an organization.

  • Process Management

    All organizations have processes, which need planning, monitoring, measurement and a strategy for continued operation and improvement.

  • Procurement Management

    Managing the sourcing, bidding, buying and other business activities to procure goods and services for an organization.

  • Program Management

    Program management aims to improve process and performance for any set of related projects.

  • Project Management

    The coordination of all of the activities, resources, and information that drive an individual, team, or organization to successfully complete projects.

  • Recruitment

    v.1 Handling the identification and outreach to job candidates for an organizaton.

    v.2 Recruitment is in essence the marketing of an organization to potential job candidates. Recruiting efforts should effectively communicate the core values of the company with the goal of attracting and engaging candidates in the hiring process.

  • Registration

    A subset of event management, registration typically involves process of adding a person to an event and includes the gathering of personal and financial data to complete the transaction.

  • Research and Development

    Activities that focus on innovation, whether generalized discovery or development of new products and services.

  • Royalties Management

    Royalties management often involves tracking income, contracts, audits and various reports. Custom software can help develop, manage and track such data.

  • Sales

    Supports and often leads an organization's revenue generation, working with clients/customers and potentially other sales channels such as distributors, partners and e-commerce.

  • Scheduling

    All kinds of things - people, events, assets, spaces, projects, tasks - can be scheduled in relationship to one another. Scheduling serves as a time-based resource for planning, reporting, and executing.

  • Security

    Security is now a discipline applied across all levels of an organization, from personnel and systems through risk management and compliance.

  • Software Engineering

    Is the process of gathering and documenting requirements and then iteratively building, testing and deploying a software product to meet the requirements.

  • Space Planning

    An activity where the requirements of individuals, teams and interacting teams is taken into account in the design and arranging of space, typically in offices.

  • Strategic Planning

    Defining goals, setting direction and planning strategy for an organization to pursue its vision.

  • Studio Management

    Studios that support artistic or creative activities need tools and processes to help manage scheduling, tasks, finances, personnel and more.

  • Supply Management

    Involves the research and acquisition of materials to be consumed in manufacturing or resold as products. Management of suppliers, inventory and risk are key components of supply management.

  • Surveys

    Used to gather information from individuals for the purpose of providing analysis to support a decision making process.

  • Telecommunications

    The transmission of audio, visual and digital information over wires and television/radio waves.

  • Time Tracking

    The documentation of time spent on tasks and by individuals, departments and whole organizations. Time tracking can interface with billing, payroll and productivity analysis.

  • Training

    You offer trainings -- we can help manage that for you.

    v2 Training is the process of offering new skills and information to individuals and teams with the goal of increasing performance and productivity

  • Transportation

    Handles the functions that bridge between other enterprise resources including supply chain and distribution.

  • User Support

    Includes a broad array of functions related to helping internal or external users of systems or services.

  • Vendor Management

    Vendor management is a class of systems and supporting software that allows customer to manage their relationships with vendors. The goal is for the customer to be able to take control of their part of the relationship, ensuring that they have an array of choices available when choosing vendors to work with.

  • Video and Audio production

    Creative departments and firms produce content for movies, videos, music and many multimedia experiences. Custom software can help manage the projects, resources and logistics for this type of production.

  • eCommerce

    eCommerce is the process of selling goods and services online. It interfaces with many areas, including web development, transaction processing, inventory control, funds transfer, marketing and data management.
